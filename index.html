    About Us

    The Apostolic Church of St. Thomas Christians traces its beginnings to the arrival of St. Thomas the Apostle in Kerala in A.D. 52. Over centuries, it developed through links with the East Syrian tradition and later grew into an autonomous Metropolitan See.

    • Rooted in early Christian traditions shaped by East Syrian ecclesial influence.
    • Maintained a historic hierarchical connection with the Patriarchate of Seleucia-Ctesiphon.
    • Evolved into a distinct, self-governing Metropolitan Church within the wider Christian communion.

    The Archeparchy of Changanacherry stands as a beacon of faith and unity, fostering community growth through evangelization, education, and service. Its rich traditions continue to guide believers in their spiritual journey and commitment to the Gospel.

    Our Leading Heights

    Pope Leo XIV

    Holy Father

    The first Augustinian Pope, Leo XIV is the second Roman Pontiff - after Pope Francis - from the Americas.

    Mar Raphael Thattil

    Major Archbishop of the Syro Malabar Church

    Mar Raphael Thattil was born to Ouseph and Thresia on 21 April 1956 and was baptized on 30 April 1956 in Thrissur.

    Mar Thomas Tharayil

    Archbishop

    Mar Thomas Tharayil (born 2 February 1972) is the Metropolitan Archbishop of the Syro-Malabar Archeparchy of Changanassery.

    Mar Joseph Perumthottam

    Archbishop Emeritus

    Born on July 5, 1948, at Kongandoor (Punnathura Parish), Joseph (Babychan) had his education in St. Thomas L. P. School Kongandoor.
